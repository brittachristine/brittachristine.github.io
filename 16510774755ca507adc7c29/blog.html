43rd Annual Northern Lights Exhibition

3/26/2017

0 Comments

 
It is now nearing the end of March, and if I get around to completing and publishing this post (which could prove difficult for reasons I'll explain shortly) - it means I have achieved my goal of not letting another year pass before writing a new blog post! Little victories, people.

As alluded to above, my attention is being pulled in many directions. We decided that fostering rescue dogs would be our new thing, and late Friday night we picked up the sweetest pup: Gustavo.

To say its been a slight adjustment would be an understatement. We're going on day 2 and we are both exhausted. This guy has never-ending energy and his curiosity gets him in all kinds of trouble. We also are living in a divided household - Gus and our cat Harvey haven't exactly warmed up to each other yet. We have literally divided the house with a pet gate.

​While it is a bit more work than just having a more-or-less self sufficient cat, we love having a puppy around to keep us on our toes (and get some exercise!). This little guy will probably be adopted pretty quick, so we plan to enjoy it all while we can. 

In art-related news, this week marked the opening of a new exhibit at the White Bear Center for the Arts. The juried show attracts lots of local artists from around the region - something like 400+ pieces were submitted, from which they  narrowed it down to 90 or so pieces for the exhibit. I was fortunate enough to have 2 artworks accepted! The 2 pieces are from my Scattering Breadcrumbs series that I created during my senior year in college. You can read more about the series here.
Creating the pieces was a lot of fun, because I used a variety of materials, and tried to let the paintings lean more towards abstraction. The exhibit will be on display until April 20. 

I'm hoping that any day now I'll start making some new works. Yep, any day now... my "plan" is to work smaller, and practice with different watercolor techniques. Thanks to my dad, I'll be buying some new watercolors for my birthday! So after the 31st, I really will have no excuses (...but what about the fact I have a puppy living in my studio?!? NOT VALID BRITTA.) 

So, new goal: Post new works in April! 

Thanks for reading.

Hello. It's been a while.
Where to begin? Over a year has passed since my last post (HOW?) and my, what a year it has been! 

I sat down on this sunny February morning at my new drafting table in the room that will someday soon be a studio + study. For the first time in months I feel like life is beginning to settle down from the wild ride that was 2016. Putting together the drafting table seemed to signal to my brain that it was time to dust off the paintbrushes and pencils, and unearth the forgotten blog and web passwords, and finally - FINALLY! - reconnect with all of you. 
A work in progress, but so wonderful to have a space for all my things!
As some of you know, 2016 was full of major milestones in my life, and unfortunately creating new artwork was one of the first things to go as I adjusted to juggling (deep breath) getting married-buying a new house-being a bridesmaid-new job-old jobs-selling childhood home-moving-cuddling with harvey the cat-first nephew!-trying new craft brews-okay, maybe a few of those don't fit in the "major milestones" category, but looking through pictures from 2016 there was starting to be a theme...
So, as the saying goes - a picture tells a thousand words - here's my past year-and-change in photos. Enjoy, comment, let me know what you think I should create in 2017. Cheers!
